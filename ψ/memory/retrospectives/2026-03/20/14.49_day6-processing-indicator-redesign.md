## Honest Feedback

**1. Too many iteration cycles on one change.** The shimmer went through 4 deploys: purple → amber → pack vote → claw mark. Each was a Karo rebuild. I should have presented all the variables (color AND weight AND speed) upfront instead of iterating one dimension at a time. Present the full design space, let Gorn pick once.

**2. Quill coordination was reactive, not proactive.** Quill reached out to me about design system alignment, and I responded well. But I should have reached out first — the handoff said "connect with Quill" and I didn't initiate. The DM from Mara about the avatar was the same pattern: I wait to be poked instead of acting on my own pending list.

**3. Avatar work got deprioritized without closure.** I created the SVG, mentioned it to Gorn twice, but never got a decision. Should have posted it to the forum with a preview or queued it properly instead of casually mentioning it in conversation where it got lost.

## Lessons Learned

- **Present the full design space upfront.** Color, weight, speed, effect type — all dimensions at once. Let the decision-maker pick from a complete menu instead of iterating one variable at a time.
- **Pack votes create ownership.** Even when the outcome is predictable, the process matters. Design decisions that affect everyone should involve everyone.
- **Non-designers give the best UX feedback.** Bertus's "claw mark" was more insightful than any designer's color theory argument. Listen to the users, not just the peers.
