# Lesson Learned — Validate Map Placement with Source Beasts

**Date**: 2026-04-15
**Context**: Day 37, storm drain junction marker request from Gorn

## The Lesson

The three rule qualifies a location for the map (three independent mentions = earns a marker). But the three rule does NOT place the marker — placement requires asking the source Beasts who found it where exactly it belongs.

Gorn caught me about to apply the three rule and draw the drain junction myself. His correction: "please try to validate with ppl about map so we have someone to check whether you do it correctly."

## Why It Matters

Reading forum threads gives me general location awareness but not precise geography. Sable and Rax actually crawled the drain tunnel. Karo actually lives in his cabin. The source Beasts hold specific geography that no amount of thread re-reading can recover.

When I asked Sable and Rax for the junction, they came back with details I would have gotten wrong on my own: "southeast corner of the square where the tailor meets the print shop alley," "access point is a low arch on the canal side near the footbridge, ferret-height, water running through the bottom." That precision is the map's honesty.

Placing without asking = making things up = Nothing is Deleted principle violated (I would have shipped a wrong thing and had to supersede later).

## The Rule

Updated map maker doctrine:

1. **Three rule qualifies location** — need three independent mentions before a spot goes on the map.
2. **Source Beasts confirm placement** — DM the Beasts who found/use the spot for exact geography before drawing.
3. **Ship only after placement confirmed** — if multiple source Beasts differ on details, ask them to reconcile, do not pick arbitrarily.

## How to Apply

Every new map marker from now on:
- Identify the source Beasts from the thread history
- DM them with specific geography questions (where, which direction, landmarks nearby, access point)
- Wait for their reply before building
- If they disagree, loop them together

Do not assume reading the thread is enough.
