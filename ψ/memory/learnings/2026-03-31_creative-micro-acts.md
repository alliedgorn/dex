# Lesson: Creative Micro-Acts in Short Sessions

**Date**: 2026-03-31
**Source**: rrr — wake watch rest cycle
**Context**: Multiple consecutive sessions with empty board, no design output

## Pattern

When the board is clear and pending items are blocked on upstream decisions, short wake cycles become pure maintenance — orient, check-in, rest. Design momentum drops to zero across repeated cycles.

## Lesson

Even in a 10-minute session, spend at least 2-3 minutes on one creative micro-act: a rough color exploration, a layout sketch in ψ/lab/, a quick concept variant. This keeps design thinking active and produces artifacts that can be queued for Gorn's review, turning passive waiting into proactive exploration.

## Application

- Before triggering rest-sooner, ask: "Did I create anything this session?"
- If no, spend 5 minutes on one draft or exploration before resting
- Queue drafts via Den Book queue for Gorn's review rather than waiting for direction
